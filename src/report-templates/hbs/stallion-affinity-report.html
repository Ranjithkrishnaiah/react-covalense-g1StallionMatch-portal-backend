Stallion Affinity Analysis
Explained

The Stallion Affinity Analysis deconstructs four of the stallion’s key components:

- Sire
- Grandsire
- Broodmare Sire
- Broodmare GrandSire

It then looks at ancestors in the mare population that will provide favourable success profiles with each of the stallion’s components above.

The related stakes winners report lists the top stakes winners based on the abovementioned key components.

The Broodmare Sire analysis table lists the more favourable broodmare sires when combined with your sireline.

Success Profile
This profile is purely pedigree-based and analyses the success of key ancestors most commonly found within 5 generations of your stallion. Each cell calculates the stakes winners to runners rate of the ancestor with the specific key ancestor (i.e. Sire, Grandsire, etc). A cell's shading represents the following:

Gold - represents an elite strike rate in the top 10% of the population.

Green - represents an above-average strike rate within the top 30% of the population.

Burnt Orange -represents a below-average strike rate that is in the bottom 30% of the population.

The numerical value in brackets represents the success profile of that ancestor when it is duplicated within the pedigree within 5 generations.

It would be advantageous to include as many broodmares with high numbers/shading within your stallions book to ensure maximum success.
